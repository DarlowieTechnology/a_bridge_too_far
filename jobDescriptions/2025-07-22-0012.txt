Responsibilities: 

As an Oracle Database Administrator, you will support the establishment and ongoing management of infrastructure managed services for the client. Your responsibilities will include: 

Service Establishment & Transition: 

Assist in gathering and reviewing information related to the implementation and operation of the client’s existing Oracle database environments. 

Participate in workshops to understand existing Oracle platforms and prepare for database operations transition. 

Contribute to the creation of key database operations documentation (e.g., System Understanding Documents, Standard Operating Procedures). 

Support the team in ensuring readiness for database operations go-live in line with the service establishment plan and schedule. 

Ongoing Database Administration & Operations: 

Perform routine Oracle DBA tasks, including: 

Monitoring and health checks 

Database backups 

Performance tuning and management 

Availability and capacity management 

Installation, configuration, and patching 

Routine administration and maintenance 

Resolving production and non-production incidents with minimal downtime or performance impact 

Managing backlog of database-related tickets and service requests 

Maintain comprehensive documentation for database platforms, processes, and procedures. 

Support efforts to align database operations with DevOps and Agile principles. 

Assist in automating database management and operational tasks. 

Collaborate with other DBAs and infrastructure teams (compute, storage, network, datacentre, and public cloud). 

Ensure security best practices are followed to meet federal government compliance standards. 

Stay updated with industry trends and technologies to contribute to continuous improvement and innovation. 

Provide input on current database platforms for public cloud migration projects. 

Knowledge of MSSQL database administration desirable, but not a requirement 

Qualifications: 

Around 3 years of experience in Oracle database administration, both on-premises and in public cloud (preferably Oracle Cloud or AWS RDS for Oracle). 

Familiarity with IT Service Management (ITSM) and ITIL frameworks. ITIL v4 certification is preferred or must be obtained within 8 weeks of starting the role. 

Strong troubleshooting and problem-solving skills. 

Good written and verbal communication skills for stakeholder engagement and technical documentation. 

Ability to work independently as well as collaboratively within a team. 

Detail-oriented mindset with a commitment to quality and continuous improvement. 


This role is ideal for someone looking to build on their database administration experience and grow within a structured service delivery environment.
